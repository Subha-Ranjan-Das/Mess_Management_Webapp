HOSTELS RULES AND REGULATIONS

There are separate hostels for the boys and girls. There are sepearte hostels for Under-graduate and Post-graduate students. Both Under-graduate and Post-graduate hostels have there own mess to provide adequate amount of food to each and every student. Kindly read the information about the hostels with regard to the following topics and follow them in order to have a pleasant stay at the hostels.

SAFETY AND SECURITY

Members of the hostel are required to read and follow the safety and security restrictions with regard to accomodation.

  • Though security arrangements are provided, the Institute shall not be responsible for any loss or damage to property belonging to the residents of the hostel.
  • The occupant will use his or her own lock and key for locking the room and will be responsible for the safety of the belongings.
  • The key of the room shall always be in possession of the member and handing over the key to any non-member or for the casual use by friends is strictly prohibited.
  • Cooking in the rooms is strictly prohibited.
  • Heaters or similar electrical appliances should not be used in the rooms.
  • No electrical appliance of more than 1000W should be used in the room.
  • No extra furniture or electrical fittings are allowed in the rooms.
    •

    ROOM ALLOTMENT

    As discussed at the start of joining the room is shared on the double basis. In case for any genuine reason, a hosteler wishes to change his/her room, folow the folowing guidelines accordingly.

  • Hostel members should occupy only the specific rooms that is allotted to them.
  • Change of room is conducted on a monthly basis depending on the availability of vacant rooms.
  • Each member can change room only once in 3 months.
  • Members who have been allotted new rooms will have to shift to the new accommodation within 2 days from the date of allotment and hand over the possession of the old room or cancel the new allotment.
  • Members shall shift/vacate the rooms occupied by them when a notice is issued by The Resource Management Officer, UG and PG Hostels.
    •

    DO's

    Students are encouraged to DO the following:

  • Always keep the telephone number of security handy in order to contact during emergencies.
  • Members should exercise all possible economy in the use of water and electricity.
  • Switch off the lights and fans when you leave the room.
  • Members should keep their rooms clean and litter free.
  • Members should take responsibility of keeping common areas like corridors, bathrooms, common rooms, game rooms tidy and litter free.
  • Keep your room clean and tidy.
  • Keep the campus clean.
  • Pay your hostel and mess bills on time.
  • Ensure that freshers feel at home.
  • Have a watch on outsiders using the mess.
  • Watch out for outsiders in hostel premises
  • Maintain discipline in the campus.
  • Park the cycles/two wheelers only at identified parking spaces.
  • Help the hostel administration in helping you.
    •

    DON'Ts

    Students are encouraged to NOT TO DO the following:

  • The Government of India has banned all types of "Ragging". It is considered as a serious cognizable offense. There shall not be any type of ragging in the campus.
  • Cooking of any type of food in the rooms is strictly prohibited.
  • Consumption/storage of alcoholic beverages in the hostel premises is an offense. It is not permitted.
  • Smoking is strictly prohibited in the campus.
  • Women visitors are not allowed in the men's hostel. Similarly, male visitors are not allowed in women's hostel.
  • Members should not throw garbage/litter from balcony or windows.
  • Do not waste food, water and electricity.
  • Do not take newspapers from common rooms to your room.
  • Do not play in the hostel corridors.
  • Do not entertain guests in room without official permission.
  • Do not disturb your room mate and neighbours by your actions.
  • Do not take plates and cups outside the dining hall of the messes.
